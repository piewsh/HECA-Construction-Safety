What is HECA?

The High-Energy Control Assessment is a structured methodology for evaluating construction site safety beyond traditional checklists

DC

Direct Controls

Engineered barriers that physically prevent energy release, guardrails, lockout/tagout systems, shoring, and anchored lifelines. Not PPE, training, or signage.

HE

High-Energy Hazards

Hazards capable of causing serious injury or death: falls above 4 ft, excavations beyond 5 ft, suspended loads, and any energy source exceeding the 500 ft-lb threshold.

%

HECA Exposure Rate

The percentage of high-energy hazard observations without a Direct Control present, quantifying a project's overall exposure to serious injury risk at any point in time.

1

Identify Energy Sources

Categorize each hazard by energy type: Gravity, Mechanical, Electrical, Motion, Pressure, Chemical, or Thermal

2

Assess Energy Level

Determine if the energy exceeds the serious-injury threshold (High vs. Low energy classification)

3

Evaluate Direct Controls

Check if a physical, engineered barrier is present that stops the energy from reaching a worker

4

Classify Outcome

Success = Direct Control present. Exposure = absent. The HECA Score is the overall exposure rate
